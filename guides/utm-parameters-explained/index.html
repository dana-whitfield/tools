UTM Parameters, Explained Simply

Updated July 2026 · by Loomwood Digital

You post the same link on Instagram, in your newsletter, and in a paid ad. A week later, analytics says you got 400 visits. From where? Without tagging, you'll never know — most of it lumps into "direct" or a vague "social". UTM parameters fix that: they're small tags added to the end of a URL that tell your analytics exactly which post, email, or ad each visitor clicked.

A tagged link looks like this:

https://yoursite.com/sale?utm_source=instagram&utm_medium=social&utm_campaign=spring_sale

The five parameters

ParameterAnswersExamples
utm_source (required)Where exactly is the link?instagram, newsletter, google, partner-blog
utm_medium (required)What kind of channel is it?social, email, cpc, referral
utm_campaign (required)Which push/initiative?spring_sale, launch_2026, black-friday
utm_termWhich paid keyword?running+shoes (mostly for search ads)
utm_contentWhich variant/creative?hero-button, footer-link, video-ad-b

Source vs. medium trips everyone up at first. Think of it this way: medium is the aisle, source is the shelf. "Email" is a medium; "newsletter" vs "receipt-footer" are sources. "Social" is a medium; "instagram" vs "pinterest" are sources.

Naming rules that keep your data clean

  • Lowercase everything. Analytics counts Instagram and instagram separately. One casing rule ends that problem forever.
  • No spaces. Use underscores or dashes (spring_sale), and pick one and stick with it.
  • Be consistent across the team. If one person writes utm_source=ig and another utm_source=instagram, your Instagram traffic is split in half. Keep a shared list of approved names.
  • Don't tag internal links. Clicking a UTM-tagged link on your own site restarts the visitor's session with the new source, erasing where they really came from. UTMs are for links that live outside your site.

Where UTM links pay off

  • Newsletters: see which issue and which link position actually drive clicks.
  • Social bios and posts: separate profile-link traffic from individual posts.
  • Paid ads: compare creatives with utm_content=ad-a vs ad-b.
  • Collaborations: give each partner their own utm_source and know exactly what each one sent you.

Frequently asked questions

Do UTM parameters hurt SEO?

Not when used on external/shared links, which is their purpose. Avoid them on internal navigation.

Are they case-sensitive?

Yes. Email and email become two rows in your reports. Go all-lowercase.

Which parameters are required?

Source, medium, and campaign are the working minimum; term and content are optional refinements.

Where do I see the results?

In your analytics tool — e.g. Google Analytics traffic-acquisition reports, broken down by session source/medium and campaign.
