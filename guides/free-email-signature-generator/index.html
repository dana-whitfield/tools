How to create your signature

  1. Open Signature Studio and fill in your name, title, company, and contact details. The preview updates as you type — leave any field blank and it's simply omitted.
  2. Pick a layout and accent color that match your brand.
  3. Click Copy. The signature is copied as rich HTML, ready to paste.
  4. Paste it into your email app — instructions for Gmail, Outlook, and Apple Mail are below.

Adding it to Gmail

  1. Gmail → gear icon → See all settings → General tab.
  2. Scroll to Signature → Create new, name it, and paste (Ctrl/Cmd+V).
  3. Set it as the default for new emails, then Save changes at the bottom.

Adding it to Outlook

  1. Outlook on the web: Settings → Mail → Compose and reply → paste under Email signature.
  2. Desktop Outlook: File → Options → Mail → Signatures… → New → paste → OK.

Signature Studio outputs inline-styled, table-based HTML — the only kind of markup Outlook renders reliably — so what you see in the preview is what your recipients get.

What makes a signature look professional

  • Less is more. Name, role, company, one phone number, one link. Six lines of banners and quotes read as noise.
  • No images for text. Image-only signatures get blocked by default in many clients and look blurry on retina screens. Use real text (images are fine as a small logo or headshot).
  • One accent color. A single brand color on your name or divider looks intentional; a rainbow doesn't.
  • Test in the wild. Send yourself a message in Gmail and Outlook before rolling it out.

Why table-based HTML?

Because Outlook's rendering engine ignores most modern CSS. Tables with inline styles are the one approach that renders consistently everywhere.

No. The tool runs entirely in your browser; your details never touch a server.
